Private AI Investment Modeling is the practice of quantifying and projecting the full cost structure of deploying AI in an on-premises or private cloud environment, as opposed to relying solely on hosted third-party AI APIs. It encompasses hardware Bills of Materials (BoMs), cloud infrastructure costs, operational expenditure (staffing, maintenance, energy), and ROI or payback-period analysis.

## Why it matters

Enterprises evaluating private AI face a multi-dimensional cost trade-off: upfront capital expenditure on GPUs and networking, ongoing operational costs, and the comparative savings or risks relative to consuming public AI APIs. Sound investment modeling quantifies each dimension before commitment.
