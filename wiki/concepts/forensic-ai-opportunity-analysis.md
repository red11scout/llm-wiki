Forensic AI Opportunity Analysis is a research discipline that goes beyond surface-level AI assessments to deliver deep, evidence-based identification, validation, and prioritization of enterprise AI use cases — specifically with the financial rigor required by CFOs and financial decision-makers.

## Core characteristics

- **Discovery depth**: Exhaustive review of business processes, data assets, and competitive signals to surface AI use cases not visible in standard assessments.
- **Validation rigor**: Each candidate use case is tested against feasibility, data readiness, and organizational constraints before being surfaced.
- **Financial prioritization**: Opportunities are ranked using quantified financial metrics — cost reduction, revenue impact, time-to-value — rather than qualitative scoring alone.
- **CFO orientation**: Outputs are framed in finance-native language (ROI, NPV, payback period) to support capital allocation decisions.

## Relationship to standard assessment

Standard [[blueally-pipeline]] assessment ([[researchapp]] → [[aiworkflow]]) produces structured use-case JSON across dimensions. Forensic analysis, as implemented in [[compass]], adds a deeper research layer on top of that foundation — validating and stress-testing opportunities before they proceed to architecture and investment planning.

## BlueAlly implementation

[[compass]] is the primary BlueAlly platform operationalizing this concept. It complements financially adjacent tools [[atlas]] and [[blueally-private-ai-investment-planner]], which address investment intelligence and infrastructure cost modeling respectively.

Together, these tools support the [[private-ai-investment-modeling]] discipline and provide CFOs with a complete analytical chain from opportunity discovery through to infrastructure cost and ROI analysis.
